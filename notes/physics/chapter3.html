Physics deals with a great many quantities that have both size and direction, and it needs a special mathematical language, the language of vectors, to describe those quantities.

3-1 VECTORS AND THEIR COMPONENTS

Scalars and Vectors

Scalars are physical quantities with magnitude only. eg: temperature (10Â°C).
Vectors are physical quantities with both magnitude and direction. eg: displacement (5 m, north).

Adding Vectors Geometrically

The sum \(\vec{s}\) of vectors \(\vec{a}\) and \(\vec{b}\) is given by \[\vec{s} = \vec{a} + \vec{b}\] where \(\vec{s}\) is a vector the starting position of \(\vec{a}\) and the final postion of \(\vec{b}\)

Properties of vector addition

  1. Commutative - the order of addition does not matter. \[\vec{a} + \vec{b} = \vec{b} + \vec{a}\]
  2. Associative - when there are more than two vectors, we can group them in any order as we add them. \[(\ \vec{a} + \vec{b}\ ) + \vec{c} = \vec{a} + (\ \vec{b} + \vec{c}\ )\]
